# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Rawkode Academy serves cloud-native engineers, platform teams, maintainers, technical founders, DevRel teams, developer marketers, product leaders, and sales engineering teams. Visitors arrive with practical intent: watch a real build, understand a technology tradeoff, follow a learning path, inspect a maintainer-led project, or decide whether Rawkode can help a DevTool or infrastructure company sharpen developer adoption.

The primary audience values practitioner evidence over polished claims. They are often evaluating Kubernetes, platform engineering, observability, security, developer tooling, AI infrastructure, and adjacent cloud-native systems under real constraints.

## Product Purpose

Rawkode Academy exists to make complex cloud-native systems easier to learn without pretending they are simple. The website is the public surface for videos, shows, articles, news, courses, learning paths, technology hubs, games, and organization partnerships.

Success means visitors can find high-signal technical material quickly, trust the source, understand what to watch or read next, and see Rawkode as a credible practitioner-led partner for developer education and adoption work.

## Brand Personality

Practical, candid, technically rigorous.

The voice should feel like a strong engineering field journal: specific, earned, direct, and curious. It should expose real work, name tradeoffs, respect maintainers, and avoid flattening infrastructure into generic marketing claims.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like slideware, a generic SaaS landing page, a paid-media publication, or a content farm. Avoid vague developer-marketing language, fake simplicity, template card grids, invented proof, broad consulting-retainer framing, and claims that Rawkode replaces a customer's marketing, product, DevRel, or sales engineering team.

For the organization surface, avoid fractional DevRel positioning. Partnerships are bounded advisory and signal products around developer adoption, not outsourced execution, lead generation, guaranteed coverage, or editorial placement.

## Design Principles

1. Show the work. Prefer concrete sessions, artifacts, maintainers, examples, and decision paths over abstract claims.
2. Keep signal dense but navigable. The site can hold a large library, but each surface needs a clear path to the next useful action.
3. Respect practitioner skepticism. Copy should name constraints, tradeoffs, and boundaries instead of promising effortless transformation.
4. Preserve editorial trust. Commercial surfaces must not blur into paid coverage or endorsement.
5. Make complexity approachable. Help visitors build intuition through sequence, context, and repeated exposure, not through oversimplified messaging.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard access, focus visibility, and semantic structure. Critical reading, discovery, and contact paths should remain usable without client-side JavaScript. Motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; animated or interactive enhancements should not hide required content. Color cannot be the only indicator of state, category, or action.
