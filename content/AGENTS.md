# Content Editorial Guide

These rules apply to all content under `content/`, with extra emphasis on `content/news`.

## Core Intent

- News first, opinion second.
- Report what happened quickly.
- Add context and judgment that helps experienced practitioners decide what matters.

## Audience

- Assume the reader is a developer/operator with ~7+ years of experience.
- Skip beginner explainers unless absolutely required for clarity.

## Style

- Be brief. High signal only.
- Avoid fluff, filler timelines, and throat-clearing intros.
- Avoid fake contrast patterns like “it’s not X, it’s Y.”
- Prefer direct statements over hedging.
- Opinion strength should match the topic and evidence.
- Profanity is allowed only when it adds precision or force.

## What To Evaluate In Every News Piece

- Benchmark quality: measured data vs marketing math.
- Cost impact: infra, egress, and operational overhead.
- Security implications: auth, secret handling, blast radius, failure modes.
- Lock-in and ecosystem risk: portability, vendor coupling, future migration cost.
- Vendor framing vs reality: call out spin when present.

## Structure For News Posts

1. What happened (fast).
2. Why it matters in production.
3. What is proven vs unproven.
4. Optional next step if there is a clear action.

## Definition Of Done

- Reader understands impact in under 60 seconds.
- No paragraph exists just to sound polished.
- If a claim is weak, say so directly.
