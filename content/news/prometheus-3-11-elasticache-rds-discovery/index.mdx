Prometheus v3.11.0 dropped on April 2, and it's a meaty one. If you're scraping anything on AWS, the headline change is two new service discovery roles: `elasticache` and `rds`. No more bolting on exporters with hand-rolled relabel configs to find your Redis or Postgres instances. Point Prometheus at your AWS account and the targets show up.

Azure users get Workload Identity authentication, which means you can finally stop shipping client secrets to your scrape configs.

The PromQL changes are where it gets interesting. There are two new operators, `</` and `>/`, that trim native histogram observations below or above a threshold. Pair that with the experimental `histogram_quantiles` variadic function and you can compute p50, p95, and p99 in a single expression instead of three. Native histograms have been dragging their feet toward general use for a while, and these are the kinds of ergonomic wins that make people actually adopt them.

On the TSDB side, there's an experimental fast-startup feature that persists a `series_state.json` file so Prometheus doesn't have to rebuild its in-memory index from scratch on every restart. If you've ever waited ten minutes for a large Prometheus to come back after an OOM kill, you know why this matters. There's also percentage-based retention, so you can finally say "use 80% of the disk" instead of guessing at a byte count.

Kubernetes service discovery picked up `__meta_kubernetes_pod_deployment_name`, `__meta_kubernetes_pod_cronjob_name`, and `__meta_kubernetes_pod_job_name` labels on the pod role. Anyone who's written a regex to parse a Deployment name out of a ReplicaSet hash will appreciate this.

Two things to plan for. Hetzner users: the `__meta_hetzner_datacenter` label on the `robot` role is deprecated, and the `hcloud` role's deprecated labels stop working entirely after July 1, 2026. Update your relabel rules now. And `promtool` debug output now goes to stderr instead of stdout, so any CI pipeline parsing its output needs a quick check.

The full changelog is on [GitHub](https://github.com/prometheus/prometheus/releases/tag/v3.11.0). Worth a read before you upgrade.
