If you've ever watched 200 GPU nodes hammer the Hugging Face CDN at the same time, you know the problem. Pulling DeepSeek-R1 onto a fleet hurts. Dragonfly just shipped a fix that doesn't ask you to stand up a proxy or rewrite a single URL.

The CNCF graduated project added two new built-in backends: `hf://` for Hugging Face Hub and `modelscope://` for ModelScope. They land via PRs #1665 and #1673, and the syntax is exactly what you'd hope for:

```bash
dfget hf://deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-R1/model.safetensors -O ./model.safetensors
dfget modelscope://deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-R1 -O ./DeepSeek-R1/ -r
```

Authentication works through `--hf-token` and `--ms-token`. You can pin revisions, recurse into whole repositories with `-r`, and pull from private repos. Nothing exotic.

## Why this changes the math

The number Dragonfly's team published is hard to ignore. A 130 GB model fanned out to 200 nodes normally drags 26 TB across the origin. With piece-based P2P streaming, one seed peer fetches it once and the other 199 nodes trade chunks among themselves. Origin traffic drops to roughly 130 GB. That's a 99.5% reduction, and it's the difference between a model rollout that finishes before lunch and one that gets you a polite email from your CDN provider.

The streaming part matters too. Peers don't wait for the seed to finish before they start sharing. As soon as a piece lands, it's available to the swarm.

## What was painful before

Before this, running Dragonfly in front of Hugging Face meant configuring it as an HTTP proxy and rewriting `huggingface.co` URLs in your tooling. Doable, but every team reinvented the same glue. Now `hf://` and `modelscope://` are first-class citizens inside the pluggable backend system, and recursive downloads preserve auth context by emitting native protocol URLs back into the pipeline instead of leaking them as raw HTTPS.

If you're already running Dragonfly for container image distribution, you can point it at model weights tomorrow. If you're not, this is a reasonable excuse to look at it. Pulling LLMs at scale isn't getting cheaper, and the bandwidth bill is a real line item now.

One question worth asking your platform team: what's your current p99 model pull time, and how much of it is just the origin throttling you?
