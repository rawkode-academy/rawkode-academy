The CNCF Technical Oversight Committee promoted Microcks from Sandbox to Incubating on May 7, 2026, nearly three years after the project entered Sandbox on June 22, 2023.

## What Microcks is

Microcks turns API contract documents into live mock servers. It speaks OpenAPI, AsyncAPI, gRPC/Protobuf, GraphQL, Postman collections, and SOAP/WSDL — and that multi-spec coverage across both synchronous and event-driven APIs is the differentiator the CNCF cited against single-protocol mocking tools.

## The numbers behind the move

Figures from the CNCF announcement:

- 2.5M+ container image downloads in 2025, roughly 3× the 2024 total.
- 34 organizations publicly listed as adopters.
- 167 active contributors from 35 organizations during 2025; 645 total lifetime contributors.
- 51 active contributors in the last quarter, with 57% retention.
- 11-day average issue resolution; ~288 new pull requests per month over the trailing year.

Current stable release is v1.14.0.

## Why this matters

Incubating status from the CNCF TOC is shorthand for "governance, adoption, and security posture are mature enough to bet a production architecture on." For platform engineering teams maintaining a golden path that includes contract testing, Microcks is now in the same maturity tier the CNCF used to gate projects like Argo, Helm, and OpenTelemetry before graduation — useful if you've been waiting for a single mocking server to cover REST, async, and RPC across the org.

**Source:** [Microcks becomes a CNCF incubating project](https://www.cncf.io/blog/2026/05/07/microcks-becomes-a-cncf-incubating-project/) — Katie Gamanji (CNCF TOC), May 7, 2026.
