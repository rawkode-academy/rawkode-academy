NetEase Games published a CNCF end-user post on May 21, 2026 describing how it cut LLM inference cold-start time from 42 minutes to under 30 seconds on Kubernetes by routing model loads through Fluid, the CNCF incubating data-orchestration project.

## The numbers NetEase reports

The team reports model load time dropped from 42 minutes against cross-region direct storage access, to 14 minutes with a traditional Alluxio cache, to 3 minutes once Fluid's prefetching workflow was enabled, to roughly 1 minute average — with some inference services landing under 30 seconds — after further production tuning. The workloads are 70B-class models that pull "hundreds of gigabytes of weights" per pod startup.

## What Fluid is doing

Fluid sits between the dataset and the runtime: it injects sidecars through a webhook, exposes data via CSI, and provides data-aware scheduling so pods land where the warm cache is. The dataset abstraction is pluggable across JindoCache, Alluxio, and JuiceFS as the underlying runtime. Scheduled and event-driven prefetching warms the cache before pods need the weights. A single-namespace model cache is shared across teams, which removes duplicate copies of the same weights across the cluster.

## Framework integration

The post calls out specific integration with vLLM and SGLang for framework-level optimizations, and notes the pattern is used for inference services that span multiple regions.

## Why this matters

The bottleneck for elastic LLM serving on Kubernetes is rarely scheduling — it is moving hundreds of gigabytes of weights to a fresh node before autoscaling pays off. NetEase's numbers are self-reported, but the recipe is concrete and the underlying tools are open source.
