The Cloud Native Computing Foundation announced OpenTelemetry's graduation at the Observability Summit in Minneapolis on May 21, 2026, lifting the project from incubating status six years after it was formed by merging OpenTracing and OpenCensus.

## What graduation required

Graduation followed a third-party security audit covering the OpenTelemetry Collector and other core components, plus a formal governance review. TOC sponsors Emily Fox and Davanum Srinivas conducted the technical due diligence. The audit and governance scope is the same bar Kubernetes, Prometheus, Envoy, and the other graduated projects had to clear.

## Adoption metrics CNCF cited

CNCF's announcement reports more than 12,000 contributors from over 2,800 companies, and ranks OpenTelemetry second in project velocity across the 240+ CNCF projects — behind only Kubernetes. The JavaScript and Python API packages each surpassed 1.3 billion downloads in the past twelve months, with both setting new monthly download records in April 2026.

## Signal status

Traces, metrics, and logs remain the stable signals. The Profiles signal — covered in earlier OpenTelemetry releases — is still alpha, and Kotlin language support is among the more recent additions. Graduation does not change those stability levels; it ratifies the project's overall governance maturity.

## Why this matters

For organizations that gate adoption on graduated status, OpenTelemetry now sits with Kubernetes, Prometheus, and Envoy as foundational CNCF infrastructure. Vendors that previously hedged on OpenTelemetry's incubating label lose that talking point.
